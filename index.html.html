The Flying Sikh

"Discipline, hard work, will power…. My experience made me so hard that I wasn't even scared of death."

Here is a Timeline of Milkha Singh's Life :

17 Oct, 1935
Born in Rajput Sikh Family in Pakistan
Aug 1947
Escaped to India after the loss of his parents during Partition.
1951
Enlisted in the Army and was first introduced to Athletics
Nov 1956
Singh represented India in the 200m and 400m competitions of the 1956 Melbourne Olympic Games.
July 1958
Won a gold medal in the 400m competition at the 1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games with a time of 46.6 seconds
1959
Singh was awarded the Padma Shri. (one of India’s highest civilian honours)
Aug 1960
He narrowly lost the bronze medal in the 400 metres at the 1960 Olympic Games in Rome, missing out on third place in a photo finish.
1960
Beat Abdul Khaliq in 200m race at Indo-Pak meet and was awarded the title "The Flying Sikh".
1962
Milkha Singh won gold in the 400m and in the 4 x 400m relay at Asian Games, Jakarta.
18 June 2021
Milkha Singh died due to post covid implications at age of 91.
